How Much House Can You Afford in 2026?

This free calculator helps you estimate a realistic home price using the widely used 28/36 rule and current mortgage market conditions. As of April 2026, average 30-year fixed mortgage rates are hovering between 6.3% and 6.5%, depending on credit, lender, and location.

Understanding the 28/36 Rule

The 28/36 rule is a conservative guideline used by many lenders and financial experts:

  • Front-end ratio (28%): Your total housing costs (principal, interest, property taxes, and insurance — often called PITI) should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income.
  • Back-end ratio (36%): Your total monthly debt payments (housing + credit cards, car loans, student loans, etc.) should stay under 36% of gross monthly income.

Many lenders allow higher DTIs (up to 43% or more in some cases), but staying closer to 28/36 helps ensure comfortable payments and better approval odds with lower interest rates.

What Affects How Much House You Can Afford?

  • Income & Debt: Higher income and lower existing debts increase affordability.
  • Down Payment: 20% or more avoids private mortgage insurance (PMI), which adds to monthly costs.
  • Interest Rates: Even a 0.5% difference can change your monthly payment by hundreds of dollars.
  • Local Factors: Property taxes and insurance vary significantly by state and neighborhood.
  • Credit Score: Better credit = lower rates and higher approval amounts.

Practical Tips to Increase Affordability

Short-term actions

  • Pay down high-interest consumer debt first
  • Boost your credit score (pay bills on time, reduce credit utilization)
  • Save aggressively for a larger down payment

Longer-term strategies

  • Shop multiple lenders — rates can vary by 0.5%+
  • Consider a 15-year mortgage if you can afford higher payments (saves tens of thousands in interest)
  • Look into first-time buyer programs, FHA, or VA loans if eligible

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in the monthly housing cost?

The calculator estimates principal & interest + property taxes (approx. 1.2% of home value per year) + homeowners insurance (~$150/month average). This gives a more realistic "all-in" payment than principal & interest alone.

Are the results guaranteed or exact?

No. These are educational estimates only. Actual qualification depends on your full credit profile, employment, assets, lender guidelines, and local market conditions. Always get pre-approved by a licensed mortgage professional.

How do current 2026 mortgage rates affect affordability?

Rates around 6.3–6.5% mean higher monthly payments than the ultra-low rates of past years. A lower rate (e.g., through excellent credit or shopping) can significantly increase the home price you can afford.

Should I use 15-year or 30-year term?

30-year terms have lower monthly payments but cost far more interest over time. 15-year loans build equity faster and save on total interest, but require higher monthly payments.

Why is a larger down payment better?

It reduces the loan amount, lowers monthly payments, avoids PMI (if ≥20%), and shows lenders you have skin in the game — often leading to better rates.
