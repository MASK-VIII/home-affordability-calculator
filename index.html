Understanding the 28/36 Rule

The 28/36 rule is a conservative guideline used by lenders:

  • Front-end ratio (28%): Housing costs should stay under 28% of gross monthly income.
  • Back-end ratio (36%): Total debt payments should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income.

Many lenders allow higher DTIs, but staying closer to 28/36 reduces stress and improves approval odds.

What Affects Your Affordability in 2026?

  • Credit score and loan type

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in the Total Monthly Housing Cost?

Principal & interest + property taxes + homeowners insurance + HOA fees + PMI (if applicable). This gives a realistic all-in payment.

Are these results guaranteed for loan approval?

No. These are educational estimates only. Actual approval depends on your full credit profile, employment, assets, and lender guidelines. Get pre-approved by a licensed lender.

How do 2026 mortgage rates affect affordability?

Rates around 6.3%–6.5% mean higher payments than past low-rate years. Shopping for the best rate can significantly increase what you can afford.

When is PMI required?

PMI is usually required if your down payment is less than 20%. It typically adds $50–$200+ to your monthly payment.

Should I choose 15-year or 30-year term?

30-year has lower monthly payments but higher total interest. 15-year builds equity faster but requires higher payments.
