How Much House Can You Afford in 2026?

This calculator provides realistic estimates using the 28/36 rule, current mortgage rates (~6.4% for 30-year fixed as of April 2026), and typical homeownership costs. All calculations run in your browser — no data is collected or stored.

Understanding the Results

We calculate three affordability scenarios internally but display the conservative "comfortable" estimate by default. Higher debt-to-income ratios may allow more, but they increase financial risk.

Key Factors That Affect Affordability

  • Income and existing monthly debts (DTI ratio)
  • Down payment size (20%+ avoids PMI)
  • Current interest rates and loan term
  • Property taxes (~1.0% national average), insurance, and HOA fees

Practical Tips to Buy More House (or Save Money)

Increase Buying Power

  • Pay down debt to improve DTI
  • Save for a larger down payment
  • Improve credit score for lower rates
  • Shop multiple lenders

Stay Conservative

  • Factor in future expenses (maintenance, utilities)
  • Avoid stretching to the maximum
  • Consider total cost of ownership, not just the loan

Frequently Asked Questions

When is PMI required?

Private Mortgage Insurance is typically required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. It protects the lender and usually adds $50–$200+ to your monthly payment.
